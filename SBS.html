Project Overview

The Single Battery System was designed to power the entire robot from a compact and robust Li-Po source. The system uses a 24V, 8,000mAh, 10C battery pack and incorporates a Battery Management System (BMS) for safe operation and monitoring. Power distribution is split across two batteries: one dedicated to the motors and the other supplying the rest of the robot. The secondary battery is further divided via DC-DC converters into multiple rails â€” 12V digital, 12V analog, 18V sensors, and 24V pistons â€” with signal isolators at each rail to prevent ground loops and ensure clean, stable power delivery. Fuse protection and LED indicators provide basic safety and visual status monitoring.

The system was designed for robustness, modularity, and reliability under dynamic robotic conditions. It has been tested under full load, demonstrating stable operation across all subsystems, including motors, electronics, sensors, and pneumatic actuators. This architecture simplifies robot wiring, reduces potential points of failure, and ensures consistent power delivery for high-performance competition robots.

Results & Validation

  • Successfully powered all robot subsystems (motors, sensors, pistons, electronics) from a single battery solution
  • Maintained stable voltage rails across digital, analog, sensor, and piston circuits
  • Signal isolation prevented ground loops and interference, ensuring clean operation of all modules
  • Integrated BMS provided safe operation without additional controllers
  • Fully tested under competition load, demonstrating reliability, modularity, and robustness
